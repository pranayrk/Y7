##### Why do companies want to track your online activity? * Save your preferences and information, like your username or what is in your shopping cart * Show you personalized content like local weather, news stories and advertisements * Gather analytics about * which pages you visited * how much time you spent on the site * the type of device and browser you used
##### What To Do About Online Tracking? * Delete your history * Adjust your privacy settings in your browser * Opt out of personalized ads * Review website cookie notices
##### What are cookies? * **Cookies** are text files with small pieces of data that are used to identify your computer. * Data stored in a cookie is created by the server upon your connection. * This data is labeled with an ID unique to you and your computer. * The network server reads the ID and knows what information to specifically serve you.

##### What is the difference between first party and third party cookies? * First-party cookies are directly created by the website you are using. * Third party cookies are generated by websites that are different from the pages that the users are currently surfing, usually because they're linked to ads on that page. * First party cookies are generally safer than third party cookies. * Third-party cookies let advertisers track an individual's browsing history across the web.
